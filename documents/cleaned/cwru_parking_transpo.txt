Title: CWRU Parking & Transportation
URL: https://offcampus.case.edu/resources/article/1275-parking-transportation

Parking
Commuter parking is essential to your life at CWRU. The safest way to park is to purchase a parking permit. All commuter students are eligible for permits upon enrollment. They are assigned to various lots throughout the University's allocation. You may purchase your permit at Access Services at 18 Crawford Hall (in the basement). Office hours are 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Monday through Friday. For general information, contact Access Services at 216.368.2273.
Commuter Lots
Commuter Lot Locations: South Campus Lot 47 is located on Adelbert and Murray Hill behind the Art Studio. The lot is shared by a limited number of undergraduate commuters, as well as faculty, staff, and graduate/professional students. Lot S-46 is parking for undergraduate commuters, students living in the university's north campus residence complex, graduate/professional students, and faculty/staff.

Note: All valid permit holders have the option to move to alternate locations after 4:30 p.m. Monday – Friday and all hours on weekends. Refer to the parking brochure or contact Access Services (216.368.2273) for specific details on after hours parking.

Getting to the Main Campus: Students parking in Lot 47 may ride the CircleLink. Students parking in Lot S-46 may take the commuter shuttle.

Cleveland Metered Street Parking
Parking is free in front of a meter from 6 p.m. to 7 a.m. Monday through Friday and all day Saturday, Sunday and major holidays (Christmas, Independence Day, etc.).

Safe Ride Program
Safe Ride provides transportation around campus and University Circle. Police and security officers will transport students and staff in a specially marked security van from 7 p.m. until 3 a.m. daily. Those interested in using the new Safe Ride program must call the police dispatcher at 368.3000 to arrange transportation. Once passengers provide their name, pick-up location, destination, and a call back number, they will be given an approximate wait time for transportation. If an officer is unavailable for the Safe Ride program, campus police will offer a walking escort or bus schedule information as an alternative.

Security Escort Service
Students who wish to request security escorts to their vehicles may call the Case Police and Security dispatcher at 216.368.3333. The service is available 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

Public Transportation
Bus Line
Case Western Reserve University has several convenient stops along the HealthLine route, the Greater Cleveland Regional Transit Authority’s state-of-the-art bus-rapid transit (BRT) system, which offers rail-like convenience with the flexibility of a bus. The entire route connects Public Square in downtown Cleveland to the Louis Stokes Station at Windermere in East Cleveland.

HealthLine Schedule Other Local Routes on Google Maps.

About the HealthLine
The HealthLine is Cleveland’s first Bus Rapid Transit system, serving the Euclid Corridor and connecting the two largest regional employment areas, Downtown and University Circle. The $200 million projects was named the “Best” Bus Rapid Transit in North America by the Institute for Transportation and Development Policy. Its 24 hybrid-electric vehicles with doors on both sides have precision docking at 36 stations. These buses produce 90 percent fewer emissions than standard buses and have a dedicated right-of-way with higher travel speed limits. Riders can expect to be picked up every 10 – 15 minutes.

Transferring to/from other Transit Systems
Passengers transferring to RTA from a transit system outside Cuyahoga County receive one free ride on RTA if they present a valid transfer from an adjacent transit agency. Conversely, once a fare is paid on RTA for their return trip, there will be a credit offered to customers transferring to these agencies, in the amount of the RTA fare paid. For more information on out-of-county fares, please visit the RTA’s website.

Rail Line
Case Western Reserve University has two convenient stops along the Red Line, the Greater Cleveland Regional Transit Authority’s rail line that services the areas between Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, downtown Cleveland, and the Louis Stokes Station at Windermere in East Cleveland.

The first stop “Cedar – University” is located adjacent to Sears think [box], while the second stop “Little Italy – University Circle” is located on Mayfield Road, just off-campus.
